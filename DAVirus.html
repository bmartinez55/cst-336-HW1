What is a Direct Action Virus?

A Direct Action Virus is one of types of viruses that corrupts files.

This virus is considered the opposite of a Resident Virus becasue it's a non-resident virus.

It attacks the opposite way of a Resident Virus, by attaching itself on a specific type of file.

Once the file it has been attached is executed it will located other similar files to spread itself.

Luckily, this virus has low impact on a computer and can be removed easily with a anti-virus software.
